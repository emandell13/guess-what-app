Streak — broad design exploration
Eight directions, well beyond the pill
Specimens only — each treatment shown in isolation so the visual is the focus, not the surrounding layout. Streak data used throughout: "5-day win streak." React to which directions spark, and we'll develop the winner against the converged modal.
01 · Hero number
The number is the design
Big chunky digit, small label beneath. The streak becomes a small monument rather than a pill. Changes the screen hierarchy — now the streak has visual weight comparable to the outcome header.

02 · Calendar strip
Literal — the streak as days on a row
A mini strip of the last N days: filled squares for days the streak was alive, today highlighted. Makes the streak a tangible thing you can see; breaks would read as visible gaps.

03 · Rubber stamp
Like a postmark slapped on your run
Angled dashed border, monospace type, red-ink number. Feels like something the host literally stamped on your card. Higher personality; could skew novelty if used too loudly.

04 · Ribbon banner
Horizontal banner, cut-tail edges
Full-width dark strip across the top of the modal body, cut-tail edges like an actual ribbon. Gives the streak its own zone instead of sitting inline. More presence; also more commitment.

05 · Tally marks
Prisoner's-wall counting, but for wins
Actual tally marks drawn to the count. Old-school, tactile, playful. Scales poorly past ~10 (visually gets thick) but works great at small streaks when you care most.
